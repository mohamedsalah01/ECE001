Internet Of Things Applications

The list of IoT applications will grow as technology evolves in the years ahead. In the near future, IoT will likely be used with Artificial intelligence to give smart solutions. Here we introduce some important real-world IoT Examples of applications.

Smart Homes

Smart homes are most trendy among these applications. The vision of a smart home is to control home appliances including lights, water flow from taps home security and safety.

House owner gets access to control and monitor these actions from their smart devices (smartphones, tablets, laptops). Imagine a situation that you forgot to turn off a water tap, you are already out of your home. You can just turn off from your phone.

Smart Cars

Just like science fiction, can you imagine controlling all the functionalities of your car by your smartphone? Having on board a smart computer that programs all your preferences; integrated sensors to alert mechanical assistance; and even a system capable of driving the car for you? All these are not fiction anymore, within a few years you can see such cars on the road.

Smart city

Smart City or "City of the Future" is a concept that classifies technology as responsible for offering improvements in urban infrastructure to make urban centers more efficient, cheaper and better to live in.

The concept of "Smart City" addresses planning and public administration through the automation of services in a creative and sustainable way. The sectors that have been developing smart cities include government services, transport and traffic management, energy, health care, water, innovative urban agriculture, and waste management

IoT in farming

Farmers can use intelligent IoT farming applications to optimize many distinct operations, such as determining the best time to harvest crops, generating soil chemistry-based fertilizer profiles and detecting soil nutrients and moisture concentrations.

A series of sensors should be installed throughout the agriculture, sensors connected to animals can help control livestock. For example, a microchip is placed in the ox's ear to track the animal, informing its vaccine history, among other information.
